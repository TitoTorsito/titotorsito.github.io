What the headline numbers mean: EQM Risk: Where today's Bitcoin price sits inside the rainbow. 0% = extreme cheap / strongest buy zone 25% = cheap zone 50% = fair-value midpoint 75% = hot / expensive 100% = extreme bubble zone Example: EQM Risk = 30% means price is in the lower 30% of the model's valuation range. That is usually interpreted as relatively cheap. EQM 50%: The model's current fair-value midpoint for Bitcoin. If BTC price is below EQM 50%, Bitcoin is below the model's median valuation level. If BTC price is above EQM 50%, Bitcoin is above the model's median valuation level. DCA interpretation: Lower EQM Risk = buy more aggressively. Higher EQM Risk = buy less, pause, or de-risk. Example formula: DCA = Max DCA × (1 - 2 × Risk) Risk is decimal: 30% risk = 0.30 DCA = Max DCA × (1 - 2×0.30) DCA = 40% of Max DCA
